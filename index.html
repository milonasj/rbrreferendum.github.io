RBR Referendum December 11, 2018
Ensuring Our Educational Excellence
Tell Me More
Thank you for taking the time to learn more about the RBR December 11 Referendum to Ensure our Educational Excellence.

About

Red Bank Regional is a unique and exceptional school district with more diversity of curriculum than any other school district in Monmouth County, and in most of New Jersey. Many students graduate from RBR with multiple college credits due to the vigorous workload in dozens of advanced placement (AP), International Baccalaureate classes and duel credit courses offered through various universities. Our students attend some of the most selective universities in the country. Outside of the classroom, RBR students excel in 26 different sports and almost 40 diverse clubs and extra-curricular activities. RBR has been recognized for its excellence by several publications including Newsweek, Niche, The Washington Post and New Jersey Monthly Magazine.

RBR’s state designated Career Technical Educational (CTE) programs in the Visual & Performing Arts, Information Technology, Engineering, and Early Childhood Education create a richness of courses for our students, but also enable the district to enroll tuition students from all over Monmouth County. This tuition program both reduces the cost of education to our taxpayers and makes RBR’s unique and diverse offerings possible. Please visit or website at www.rbrh.org to learn more about our high school.

Why

RBR’s increased enrollment is now causing space shortages with many more students are coming from lower grades. Industry standards designate RBR’s capacity at 1,043. Our enrollment for 2018 was 1,217. Demographics studies expect that to increase to 1,395 in four years. We are simply out of space.

These space limitations have caused the school to turn back many tuition students. The recent loss of revenue has already been significant. RBR took in $4 million in tuition four years ago. Last year that amount dropped to $2.8 million. By comparison, total state aid is approximately $1 million. This means taxpayers will have to make up the difference in higher taxes or the special programs that distinguish RBR with be cut.

Additionally, the RBR building is approaching 50 years old with age-related maintenance needs. The roof has reached the end of its useful life. As the state limits the amount of capital and maintenance reserves RBR is allowed to maintain, it is impossible to ‘save up’ to do the needed work.

Our community driven strategic plan identified what the taxpayers and the public want from the school in both educational excellence and athletic facilities.

What

In order to protect and enhance RBR’s exceptional programs and to meet other facility needs, the school district will hold a referendum on December 11, 2018.  The voters in the towns of Red Bank, Little Silver, and Shrewsbury will be asked to vote on two questions. Please note the net cost figures are not final since state aid numbers will not be known until late August or early September.


Question #1 will include replacement of the roof that is at its end-of-life, renovation of existing academic spaces and addition of 10 new classrooms. This question qualifies for state debt service aid.
